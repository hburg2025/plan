First things first!

Before anything else, please:

  1. Be smart about personal information! You will notice we don't ask for any information on this site. You should be suspecious of anyone asking for detailed personal information, even if they seem like they want to help or belong to a community organization. Also, try to consider the risk that people you share sensitive personal information with could be targeted by an ICE raid themselves: is it low risk, or higher risk?

    • Don't leave sensitive information in a place where ICE could find it during a raid. [TODO, backpack story of A.]

    • As part of your plan, you may decide to use legal documents that will contain some personal information and must be signed and stamped by a notary public. Notarization helps to prove that the document was really signed by the person whose name is on it and that that person signed it willingly. In Virginia, a notary may not legally charge more than $10 to notarize a document and most banks will notarize a document for free.

      However, notaries may not give legal advice in the United States unless they are also licensed lawyers. Never ask or pay a notary for legal advice. They are not qualified to provide it, and relying on legal advice from a notary can be dangerous. You should talk with an attorney instead.

  2. Reach out for more help, as needed. If you own real estate or business property, or have any criminal background, you should definitely talk to attorney for legal help. If you have children or adults who depend on you, you may want to talk to an attorney about legal options in case you are detained or deported. [TODO: other "scope" cases?]
