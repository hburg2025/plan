You can use a legal document called power of attorney in case you are detained. This gives your "trusted helpers" more power to access your assets and accounts. Generally, you sign a form in front of a notary to give the "power of attorney" and say exactly which powers the "trusted helper" should have. It is very important to only give power of attorney to someone you trust completely, to act as you would wish.
